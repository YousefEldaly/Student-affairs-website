University History

Founded in 1908 by a committee of private citizens with royal patronage in 1908 and became a state institution under King Fuad I in 1925.
It was renamed a after the 1952 Egyptian Revolution.Its early founding and location made it a model for later universities throughout the Arab world. It was taken over as a state university in 1925 and became Cairo University in 1954. Significant contributions were made by Princess Fatma Ismail. In the early 1900s, she donated land to the university as a part of her fundraising campaign for the establishment of Egypt's first formal university It is the second oldest institution of higher education in Egypt

Cairo University Today

currently enrolls approximately 155,000 students in 20 faculties and 3 institutions. It counts three Nobel Laureates among its graduates and is one of the 50 largest institutions of higher education in the world by enrollment. one of the top universities in Africa.

Nobel laureates

Naguib Mahfouz, Nobel Prize for Literature in 1988
Yasser Arafat, Nobel Peace Prize in 1994

Recent Events

Iftar

Under the auspices of the university administration and the supervision of the college administration, and in cooperation with the Youth Welfare Department, the college students union organizes the largest iftar for students in the history of the college The main courtyard of the college is decorated with a Ramadan lantern designed by the college students through the iftar tables of more than five hundred fasting students. With fasting meals, Coptic students participate in the celebrated event in an atmosphere of love and fun. Only in guarded Egypt are the two fasts synchronized and those who fast are united in the love of their college.
